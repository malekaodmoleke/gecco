Corporate Social Environmental Responsibility Policy

CSER

How we balance the natural world, social, and economic priorities over the long term.

Policy brief & purpose

Our Corporate Social Environmental Responsibility policy explains our approach to sustainable business. We recognise our responsibility towards the natural world, society, local communities, customers, colleagues and the economy. Sustainability is one of our key company objectives and is embedded throughout our strategies and everyday decision-making.

Scope

This policy applies to our companies, suppliers and partners. It focuses on the service sector, the education and care of small children, and the training of adults.

  • How we care for people.
  • The products we buy and use to sustain our services.
  • The environment we provide to deliver our services.
  • The transport and communication systems that support our work.

Business Ethics

We conduct business with integrity and respect for human rights.

  • Safety and fair dealing.
  • Respect towards consumers and colleagues.
  • Anti-bribery and anti-corruption practices.
  • Responsible supply chain checks.
  • Reduced carbon footprint, packaging and waste.

Context

We are a responsible business that aims to meet high standards of ethics, professionalism, care and sustainability in every area of our work.

Legality

Our company respects the law, honours internal policies, ensures operations are legitimate, and keeps partnerships and collaborations open and transparent.

Governance Policy

This policy describes the framework of procedures by which the Board of Directors relates to stakeholders, including owners, financiers, management, colleagues, customers, local authorities, inspection bodies, government and the wider community.

Fairness Accountability Compliance Transparency Risk Management Strategy

Fairness & Stakeholder Involvement

Tops & Aspire promote fairness by giving stakeholders a meaningful voice in company operations. Decision-making is decentralised, encouraging directors, managers and colleagues to take responsibility and contribute based on their roles and expertise.

Engagement happens through job descriptions, action plans, meetings, appraisals, reviews and forums. Regular consultation ensures feedback informs adjustments for collective benefit.

Accountability

The company recognises accountability as a major governance challenge, ensuring colleagues actively follow policies that are researched, communicated and reinforced through leadership.

Risk Management

Strong corporate governance protects the company from fraud, scandals and liability while reinforcing its reputation as responsible, trustworthy and investable.

Transparency

The organisation is committed to honesty and openness in communication, including sustainability reports, self-assessments, financial reports, questionnaires and press releases.

Strategy

The company ensures colleagues share a common vision for the future through aims, objectives, strategies and long-term plans communicated appropriately.

Supporting Responsible Sustainable Business

GECCO supports practical action, responsible leadership and long-term decisions that protect people, communities and the planet.
