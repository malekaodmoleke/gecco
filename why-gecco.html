Our unique relationship with Tops Day Nurseries

Tops are constantly identifying and acting on areas for improvement in their day-to-day operations. GECCO is Cheryl’s vehicle for sharing what they learn with others in Early Years childcare and education.

No politics, no hidden agendas, no conflicts of interest

Cheryl, a leading advocate for sustainable business and education, gained global attention in 2017 for banning plastic glitter in Tops nurseries and replacing commercial baby wipes with homemade alternatives.

Reducing Single-Use Plastics

Small steps towards a sustainable future

Tops aim to stop or reduce the use of single-use plastics. Their work includes washable aprons, “Switch it off” stickers, parent communication by email, reduced printing, and practical savings reinvested into greener improvements.

Practical changes already making a difference

Swapped light bulbs for LED bulbs
Installed time and light sensors
Installed timers on coolers and heaters
Set printers to sleep instead of standby
Fitted fuel-saving magnets on mains
Installed solar panels at multiple sites
Made cycling to work more accessible
Identified sustainable travel options
